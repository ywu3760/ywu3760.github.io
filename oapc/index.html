What is Ocean Acidification?
Ocean Acidification describes the process of ocean water becoming more acidic over time as a result of absorbing increasing amounts of carbon dioxide.
A majority of the compound can be traced to human activity such as emissions from vehicles that use nonrenewable fuel, deforestation, and the burning of fossil fuels for energy.
On the pH scale, with a pH of 1 being the most acidic, ocean water pH has fallen by 0.1 to around 8.1 since approximately 200 years ago. A 0.1 deficit may appear small, but the pH scale is actually logarithmic which means that ocean water has gotten around 30% more acidic.
