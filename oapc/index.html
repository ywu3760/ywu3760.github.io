Introduction
Ocean Acidification describes the process of ocean water becoming more acidic over time as a result of absorbing increasing amounts of carbon dioxide. A majority of the carbon dioxide emissions can be traced to human acitivities like the ones shown to the right.
The Chemistry Behind This
Our oceans are natural carbon dioxide sinks: they are constantly absorbing carbon dioxide from our atmosphere. However, when the gas enters our oceans, it would react with the water to form carbonic acid, which acidifies our oceans. The increase in acidity is typically neglegible, but the amount of carbon dioxide being absorbed by our oceans is increasing every year. As a result, the average pH of our oceans has dropped by about 0.1 since the pre-industrial times, which is approximately a 25% increase in acidity.
